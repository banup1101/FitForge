Understanding Workout Splits

A workout split refers to how you divide your training routine among different muscle groups over the course of a week. Please select a split:

  • Specific Muscle Group: Targets one specific muscle group on different days. This allows for focused training.
  • Upper/Lower: Divides workouts into upper body and lower body sessions. It allows for more volume and intensity per muscle group.
  • Push/Pull/Legs: Separates workouts into push exercises (chest, shoulders, triceps), pull exercises (back, biceps), and leg exercises. This split provides adequate rest between muscle groups.
  • Arnold Split: Divides the workouts into three different sections. Separating it by chest and back, shoulders and arms, and leg exercises.

How Often to Workout

The frequency of your workouts depends on your fitness goals, experience level, and recovery ability. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Beginners: Aim for 2-3 total body workouts per week with at least one day of rest between sessions to allow for recovery.
  • Intermediate: 3-4 workouts per week with a mix of full-body or split routines, allowing for adequate rest days and recovery.
  • Advanced: 4-6 workouts per week, incorporating more advanced training techniques and listening to your body's need for rest and recovery.
